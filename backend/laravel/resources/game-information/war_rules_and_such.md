War rules and such

Kingdom destruction:

To fully destroy a kingdom, you must destroy all capitols and/or nuclear capitals. Once the last one falls, 100% of the kingdom is destroyed.

Partial kingdom destruction:

Destroying a capitol destroys 50% of that kingdom.
Destroying a nuclear capital destroys 75% of that kingdom.
If a kingdom has multiple capitals, losses stack proportionally until complete destruction.

War declarations:

Wars should be declared before major hostilities begin unless special circumstances or ongoing roleplay make that impractical.

Army deployment and movement:

You may only deploy units if you have available structure level cap and command center support.
Movement is measured in centimeters on the map according to each unit's movement stat.
Naval units require water.
Aircraft ignore most terrain movement restrictions.
Some special units explicitly ignore terrain.

Combat basics:

Attacks use the attacking unit's ATK against the target's DEF.
Damage is applied using the unit's DMG value when an attack succeeds.
HP tracks how much damage a unit can sustain.
Units only act as many times per round/year as allowed by ACT.
Range is measured in centimeters.

Discovery and intelligence:

Some stealth units cannot be discovered unless killed.
Academies and related systems influence learning plans, finding spies, and related intelligence actions.

On-death effects:

Some advanced units grant resources or cause area damage when destroyed. These effects happen immediately on death and should be resolved as written in the unit entry.

Aircraft carriers:

Units marked Aircraft carrier can carry and project qualifying air units as appropriate for scenario handling.

Missiles and bombs:

Units that can launch missiles or deliver bombs may do so only as specified in their unit text and within stated range limits.

Capital fortresses:

Capital fortresses are tied to nuclear capital progression. They are singular strategic assets and their destruction has major kingdom-wide consequences.

Scoring:

Each surviving capitol grants +5 score yearly.
Each surviving nuclear capital grants +20 score yearly.
Other scoring events may be handled by scenario or admin adjudication if needed.

Admin adjudication:

Where a rule interaction is unclear, resolve it consistently with the written unit/stat text first, then with admin judgment for edge cases.
